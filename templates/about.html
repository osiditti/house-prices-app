About Me

In my daily job, I give strategy advice to companies in the energy and financial sectors in the Central and Eastern European region. In my freetime, I code and experiment with machine learning techniques. The Predicting House Prices web app presents the first full-fledged data science project that I created, primarily as a final project for Harvard's CS50 course.

About Predicting House Prices

As its name suggests, the purpose of this web app is to predict the market price of a house (or more precisely, of a house in Ames, Iowa). Its backbone is a machine learning model that I have built on Kaggle's Ames House Prices dataset. Since the goal is to predict a continuous number (price), the app applies regression (rather than classification or clustering) to predict the market price based on the house's key parameters.

Machine Learning Model

The machine learning model behind the scenes is an XGBoost Regressor implemented from the sklearn API. XGBoost is an extremely powerful gradient boosting algorithm, which builds its prediction model as an ensemble of weak predictors. (It is also known as the model that wins most Kaggle competitions.)

The model's final performance on the test set is a MAE (Mean Absolute Error) of +/- 2.5% and an RMSE (Root-Mean-Square Error) of +/- 3.8%.
